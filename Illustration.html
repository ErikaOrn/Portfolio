B & D Nudes

Although not the best piece anatomy-wise, however this piece was made following a life drawing class in which i was told to keep pushing value.
Thus, instead of making my usual flat sketches, I decided to push value and make my characters feel more like figures, rather than just drawings.

Hina Portrait

One of the oldest pieces on this website, but still one of my proudest. A white charcoal portrait of one of my many dolls encased in glass. This piece helped teach me how to love developing value.

B & D Abstraction Exercise

Another post life drawing piece. In the class my instructor told us to put random lines on our paper in pastel to add not only visual interest, but also as a way to capture the energy of the model of the day.
I used that technique to capture the energy of two of my characters and proceeded to draw exaggerated and abstracted versions of them. This is also one of my only pieces I have matted, on a beautiful cyan faux-suede mat.

Free Gestures

Although this could qualify as a life drawing piece, these forms are completely from my own imagination. Thus, these aren't drawings from life, but drawings from my mind.
